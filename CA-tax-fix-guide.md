# Fixing the $889 California Bill — Quick Guide

**The problem:** $950 of the $889 owed is California's health insurance penalty (Individual Shared Responsibility Penalty). It's being charged because the return is set up as if she's a California resident. She isn't — she's a Utah resident who worked in CA for the summer.

**The fix:** Change her to a **California Nonresident** (or Part-Year Resident only if she actually established residency). The penalty should go to $0 or a small prorated amount.

---

## Step-by-step in FreeTaxUSA

1. **Log in → open the 2025 return.**

2. **Go to the "State Taxes" section → California.**
   - Look for a screen titled something like *"California Residency"* or *"Tell us about your time in California."*

3. **Change residency to Nonresident.**
   - Answer: *"Lived in another state (Utah) all year and only earned money in California."*
   - If it asks for dates, enter the summer work dates (arrival in CA → departure).
   - Do **not** mark her as a full-year CA resident.

4. **When it gets to the health insurance questions (CA section):**
   - If **Nonresident:** you should see the penalty drop to **$0** automatically. No further action needed.
   - If the system still treats her as Part-Year: say she had **minimum essential coverage** for every month she was *not* in California (the Utah months don't count for the CA penalty). If she was on your family health plan during the CA summer, mark those months covered too — the penalty goes to $0.

5. **Check the updated summary.**
   - Federal refund should stay ~$1,151.
   - CA amount should flip from "owed $889" to a small **refund** (roughly $61 — the withheld $91 minus the $30 actual tax).

6. **Before submitting, verify:**
   - Filing form is **540NR** (Nonresident/Part-Year), not **540**.
   - Only her CA-earned wages (~$5,343) are being taxed by California.
   - Individual Shared Responsibility Penalty = $0.

---

## What to have handy

- Summer job W-2 (CA employer)
- Dates she was in California
- Proof of health coverage during the summer (insurance card, or confirmation she was on your family plan)
- Her Utah address (permanent address on the return)

---

## Why this works

California's health insurance penalty only applies to people who are California residents. A Utah resident working a summer job in CA is a **nonresident** for CA tax purposes — she owes CA income tax only on the wages she earned there, and is **not** subject to the health mandate.

Even if FreeTaxUSA pushes her into Part-Year Resident, the penalty is prorated to only the months of CA residency, and is waived entirely if she had health coverage during that time (including being on a parent's plan).
